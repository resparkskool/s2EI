The Cost of "I'm Fine"

Someone asks how you're doing. You say "fine," or "okay," or "good." Most of the time that's not a lie exactly — it's just the only word that made it to the surface in time. Underneath it there was usually something far more specific waiting.

Think of emotions like color. If all you ever named was red, blue, and yellow, you'd still be looking at crimson and turquoise and golden amber — you just wouldn't have a word for what you were seeing. Most of us are operating with the emotional vocabulary we had at five years old, trying to describe an adult's inner life with a kid's color set.

You can't manage what you can't name. You can't be known through a word that hides more than it reveals.

Your Precise Sentence

This is what "fine" was standing in for
